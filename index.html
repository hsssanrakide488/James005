What is DMCA ignored hosting?


DMCA ignored hosting is a type of web hosting that doesn't adhere to the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This means that your website won't be taken down if someone complains about copyright infringement.

Why is this important?


Well, you're running a website that relies on user-generated content (like a forum or blog), then there's always the potential for someone to upload copyrighted material without your knowledge. If your host adheres to the DMCA, then your entire site could be taken down if this happens - even if it's just one user who uploaded the infringing material.

However, with DMCA ignored hosting, your site will stay up even if someone does upload copyrighted material. This is because your host doesn't have to comply with the DMCA - meaning they can't be forced to take down your site.

This is extremely important for websites that rely on user-generated content, as it gives you much more freedom and flexibility in what you can publish on your site.

Metered or unmetered bandwidth

The amount of bandwidth your site uses will directly affect the cost of your hosting plan. If you have a lot of traffic, you'll need to choose a hosting plan with more bandwidth. But if you don't have much traffic, you can save money by choosing a lower-priced hosting plan with less bandwidth.

There are two types of bandwidth: metered and unmetered. Metered bandwidth means that your host charges you based on the amount of data transfer your site uses. This can be a per-gigabyte charge or a monthly fee. Unmetered bandwidth means that your host doesn't charge you based on how much data transfer your site uses. Instead, you pay a flat monthly fee for a certain amount of bandwidth.

If you're not sure how much bandwidth your site will use, it's best to choose a dmca ignore hosting  plan with unmetered bandwidth. That way, you won't have to worry about exceeding your limit and being charged extra fees.
